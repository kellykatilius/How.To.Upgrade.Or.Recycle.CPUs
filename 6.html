Why We Should Recycle Computer Components

Computer parts have dangerous materials that can affect the environment. Few people know it has one of the biggest impacts on our environment. Electronic waste or (e-waste) only takes up 2% of our landfills but accounts for 70% of our total toxic waste production. The waste would affect the groundwater which can lead to cancer and organ damage. As electronics become more relevant today, it will lead to more waste. In 2016, there were almost 50 million tons of e-waste produced. Electronic waste still increasing today. Recycling computer parts will help benefit us.

Benefits to Recycling

If everybody contributes to recycling electronic waste it will benefit the environment and the people. Recycling electronic waste can create jobs. Reusing and recycling electronic devices can save energy consumption across the map. We need to hire people to do these tasks still. 10,000 tons of recycled material can create as many as 300 new jobs. Another benefit comes from saving resources from recycling old computer parts. Wires and other components of electronic devices contain aluminum and copper. Worker can collect the materials and reuse them. Less mining for ores and other natural resources for the new computer parts.

How to Start Recycling

The user will have plenty of ways to recycle. For example, if the user upgrades a computer part but the old component still works they can sell it online. Other people can buy your computer part and still use it. On Ebay you can either auction or list it as a price for others to buy and earn money back through paypal. Amazon also functions as an alternative place to sell or to recycle. Amazon will even ship a box for you to send them to. If a computer part stop working, the user should go to a recycling center that accepts electronic waste. Google the nearest recycling center that will accept electronics waste. Another suggestion is to google retailers near you that recycle electronic waste. Best Buy or Staples accepts electronic waste and will recycle them.
